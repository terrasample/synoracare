## Recommendation
My recommendation is to keep the handbook short, role-based, and action-oriented. It should help a new user understand what SynoraCare AI does, where to click first, and how to use the platform safely without turning into a policy wall.

The best version is a polished onboarding guide with three goals:
- explain the product in plain language
- show the core workflow by role
- reinforce safety, privacy, and escalation rules

## What SynoraCare AI Is
SynoraCare AI is a care-operations platform for healthcare and disability support teams. It brings together daily operations, documentation, workflow management, and governed AI assistance in one place.

It is designed to help teams:
- manage clients and assignments
- document care activity in the tracker
- access grounded AI support from uploaded materials
- maintain auditability, legal export, and break-glass controls

## Who This Handbook Is For
This handbook is intended for:
- Super Admins
- Org Admins
- Supervisors
- DSPs and frontline staff
- new team members during onboarding

## Core Product Areas
### Dashboard
The dashboard gives a quick operational snapshot. Summary cards should be treated as navigation, not decoration.

### Ask AI
Ask AI helps staff get grounded answers from care documents and operational context.

### Tracker
Tracker is the central activity log for care events, handoffs, and operational updates.

### Patient Workspace
Patient Workspace collects client-specific care context in one place.

### Documents
Documents is where care plans and reference materials are uploaded and classified.

### Assign
Assign connects staff members to the clients they support.

### Team
Team is used to invite users, manage roles, and control access.

### Clients
Clients stores the client roster and lifecycle details.

### Break Glass
Break Glass provides emergency access with full auditing.

### Audit
Audit shows security and operational activity for review and oversight.

### Legal
Legal supports record export and retention-aware workflows.

### Training
Training provides role-specific guidance and reminders.

## Suggested First-Time Flow
1. Set up the organization and the first admin account.
2. Invite the team and assign the right roles.
3. Add clients and connect staff assignments.
4. Upload core documents such as care plans and supporting records.
5. Use Ask AI to review grounded guidance before acting.
6. Record daily activity in Tracker.
7. Use Patient Workspace for client-specific work.
8. Monitor Audit and Legal when compliance review is needed.

## Dashboard Flow Recommendation
The dashboard should answer one question quickly: what needs attention now?

Use the four summary cards as drill-down controls:
- Clients opens the client roster
- Pending opens unresolved tracker items
- Escalated opens urgent or high-risk items
- Completed opens finished work for review and reporting

Recommended behavior:
- the whole card should be clickable
- the clicked card should open the matching filtered view
- the state should remain easy to understand when the user returns

## Daily Work Rhythm
For most users, the daily flow should feel like this:
1. Check the dashboard for outstanding work.
2. Review tracker items that need attention.
3. Open the relevant client in Patient Workspace.
4. Use Ask AI only when the answer should be grounded in uploaded material.
5. Save everything that needs to be auditable.
6. Escalate safety or compliance issues immediately.

## AI Usage Principles
Ask AI should support judgment, not replace it.

Use it when:
- you need a quick explanation of a care document
- you want a grounded summary of a client or workflow
- you need help finding the right operational step

Do not use it as the sole source of truth when:
- the situation is urgent
- a policy decision is required
- the client’s safety or legal status is unclear

## Safety And Compliance
SynoraCare AI should always be used with care, privacy, and accountability in mind.

Important rules:
- only authorized users should see sensitive data
- every important action should be traceable
- emergency access should be limited and audited
- legal exports should be controlled and reviewable
- escalations should be visible quickly to the right people

## Owner And Admin Controls
The owner should be able to see extra controls that are not shown to general users.

Recommended owner-only behavior:
- a private toggle for real data and demo data
- visibility restricted to the owner account
- admin controls added later as the organization grows

## What Good Looks Like
SynoraCare AI is working well when:
- a new user understands the platform in minutes
- the dashboard leads directly to action
- staff can complete daily work without confusion
- compliance and auditability are built into the workflow
